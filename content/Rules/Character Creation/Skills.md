# Definition
- Skills are the basic representation of any training, talent, or experience you have. 
## Allocation
- Default at **1d4**
- Allocate points to determine what die is assigned, each point steps the die up by one step.
- Start with 16 points.
### Points
- 0 Points = **1d4**
	- Untrained
- 1 Points = **1d6**
	- Competent
- 2 Points = **1d8**
	- Expert
- 3 Points = **1d10**
	- Master
- 4 Points = **1d12**
	- Grandmaster
## Skills
### Strength
#### Athletics
- Ability to perform physical actions 
	- Climbing
	- Jumping
	- Sprinting
#### Brawn
- Tolerance for prolonged physical stress
	- Carrying heavy loads
	- Experiencing harsh environmental conditions
	- Physical labor
#### Might
- Short bursts of raw physical prowess
	- Grappling
	- Melee combat
	- Short physical stress
#### Conditioning
### Speed
#### Acrobatics
- Any action requiring physical balance or equilibrium
	- Maneuvering around traps
	- Balancing on cables
	- Performing flips
#### Piloting
- Ability to naturally shift in and out of your alt mode as well as operating external vehicles.
	- Gracefully transforming following a jump (similar to rolling into a run)
	- 
#### Finesse
- Fast and/or precise application of dexterity, primarily used in close quarters combat.
#### Infiltration
- Ability to sneak around undetected and overcome basic security measures.
#### Targeting
- Accuracy and effectiveness when firing ranged weaponry.
### Smarts
#### Alterness
- Awareness of the world around including threats, clues, and opportunities. 
#### Culture
- Remembering information about groups and people including their history, customs, and traditions.
#### Science
- Understanding and application of the scientific method, calculations, and a logical path to the definitive answer.
#### Survival
- Being able to thrive in dangerous/hostile environments like determining weather the ground is stable, and area is abandoned or not, and finding resources like energon.
#### Technology
- Innovation, engineering, applied mathematics, and the research, experimentation, design, and use of computers and machinery.
### Social
#### Animal Handling
- Caring for, training, and otherwise manipulating animal life.
#### Deception
- Telling direct falsehood, misdirection, body language, and any other ways of concealing a truth.
#### Performance
- Any form of a creative outlet/art like dance, singing, painting, poetry, and acting.
#### Persuasion
- Convincing others to do things or see things from your point of view.
#### Streetwise
- Being able to navigate your way through cities and situations without getting a bind.
### Spark
#### Willpower
- Resistance to mental domination, fear, or despair. The ability to push through psychological strain with sheer determination.
#### Vitality
- Endurance of the life systems, how well a cybertronian can withstand critical damage.
#### Integrity
- Maintaining the stability and purity of one's spark under stressful situations.
	- Resisting poisonous effects like tox-en
	- Repairing internal damage
	- Staying true to your ideals
#### Ignition
- Channeling your spark energy into a burst of inspiration, power, or will.
	- Trigger a desperate action like an emergency repair or attack
	- Transform into alt mode under less than ideal conditions
	- Briefly overclock abilities at a cost
		- Add a complication to deal with later
#### Harmony
- Balancing the spark's connection between mind, body, and purpose. 
	- Stabilizing systems after critical damage
	- Meditation to focus
	- Aligning with others (combiner)
